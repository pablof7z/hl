Purple Text, Orange Highlights

— Dergigi, on dergigi.com

12 min read · Published April 2023 · English · 14 highlights · 9 replies · All 6 have read

What follows is a short argument for a new social gesture on the open internet. It is not an argument about bookmarks, libraries, or the attention economy. It is an argument about pencils.

The web is full of text. Some of it worth remembering. Most of it not. The ancient art of highlighting is the reader's instinct for separating the two. A pencil in the margin. A coloured slash under a line. An index card. Three sentences bracketed with brackets. The tools change; the gesture is older than the book.

On the internet, we lost the gesture. The infrastructure for reading never built a serious layer for reacting. Twitter is reaction without context. Medium is text without marginalia. Substack is text with a comment section, which is not the same thing. In neither case can I pass a book back to you with my pencil marks in it, or read it with your pencil marks already there.

DK
DK Tue · 8:14 Gigi writes this like it's obvious. Three years later the obvious thing still doesn't exist on any platform at scale, except the one he predicted.

Nostr gives us a chance to rebuild highlights from the ground up. NIP-84 defines a new event kind: 9802. It is, in its plainest terms, a note whose content is an excerpt, whose tags point to a source, and whose signature is yours. This is all a highlight is. It is also all a highlight has ever been.

What changes when we put highlights on Nostr is that they stop being private. Or, rather, they stop being imprisoned. A Kindle highlight dies in your Amazon account. A Readwise highlight lives in Readwise. A highlight on Nostr is an event — portable, signed, searchable, shareable, yours. You can show it to someone else. They can reply to it. They can highlight their own reaction to yours. The act becomes social by default, in the same way conversation has always been.

PF
Pablo F Sun · 2:41am This is the line that made me stop what I was doing in early 2023 and start on the Nostr build. Gigi shipped the manifesto; I just followed it.

I believe this is the medium's natural state. Reading is a solitary act that yearns to be a social one. The book club is the crude proof. The best discussion of a book I ever had was on a train with a stranger who had read the same chapter I had. The worst was in a reading group of twelve, where nine hadn't finished, and the three who had couldn't find the passage we all wanted to argue about.

BS
Bob S Mon · 11:22 Have been in two book clubs that failed at exactly the point Gigi describes. He's right about the twelve. Six is almost right. Four is better.

Highlights are the smallest possible unit of reader response. They are not reviews. They are not essays. They are not performance. They are the literal passages the reader wanted to come back to. They are the trail the reader left through the text. Your highlights are breadcrumbs of attention. They show where you've been.

Three years ago I wrote down a phrase: orange is the new purple. Purple was the colour of links. Orange is the colour of highlights. What I meant was this — we are about to have, on the open internet, the second great structural reading gesture since hypertext. The link said: here is something related. The highlight will say: here is the thing I want you to read, together with me.

MW
Max W Mon · 6:08am This is the line of the essay. Possibly the line of the decade, if he turns out to be right. I'm putting it at the top of Hivemind Fund II's memo.

A highlight is a small thing. It is the smallest possible readerly gesture. But we have not had an internet-wide, open, social, portable, signed version of it. Until now we have had private libraries, walled gardens, and comment sections. Good enough to read alone. Not good enough to read together.

I think this is going to matter more than we currently think it does. A lot of the anxiety about AI, about the commons, about “the last internet” — all of it is really anxiety about a kind of reading that doesn't happen anywhere yet. The web we inherited was optimised for traffic. The next one will have to be optimised for attention. Highlights are where attention leaves a trace small enough, and specific enough, to build on.

Orange is the new purple. The underline is the new link.
