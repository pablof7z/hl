02 · Emerging design language

What round two is quietly locking in.

Round two isn't just a second draft — it's starting to settle some elements that a broader design system could build from. We're calling these out so you can push back before they become house style.

Typography
Fraunces (a variable transitional serif) for display and emphasis; Inter for body copy; Caveat as the product's single permitted handwritten voice (reserved for member annotations); JetBrains Mono for meta-typography and technical labels.
Palette
Warm cream paper (#F7F3EB), deep ink (#15130F), and a single terracotta accent (#C24D2C) for emphasis and interactive states. A palette of soft, desaturated highlight colours for multi-member UI. One reserved color — ballpoint blue (#1F3F78) — for annotations. Nothing else chromatic.
Signature gesture
The marked phrase. A low, wavy pen underline under key sentences that reads as someone's pencil mark, not a hyperlink. It's the product's most recognisable gesture — we'd carry it into the app itself as the visual treatment of a highlight.
Voice
The marketing copy is quiet, specific, and declarative. The annotation voice is lowercase, fragmented, and personal. The contrast between the two is the product's core argument, made on the page itself.
Anti-patterns we're holding
No hero screenshots floating over headlines. No three-up feature cards. No testimonial walls. No gradients or generic SaaS motion. No emoji.
03 · What we need from you next

Three questions that move us to round three.

The reader
Is one reader in the margin the right choice, or should we A/B test a room of three (less spotlight on any single voice but more diversity of reaction)? Our current bet: one reader is more honest and more powerful. Push back if you disagree.
The podcast lane
How much do you want to lean into podcasts as a named pillar? Round two treats it as “not just books” with a working UI mock and a reader annotation. A more aggressive version could open with podcasts (“the two-hour conversation you can finally discuss”) and treat books as equally first-class after. Tell us.
The CTA promise
Currently “Request a card — opening in small waves.” Scarcity-framed. Alternate: “Start a room — bring your people.” Abundance-framed, agency-first. Which feels truer to how you want this to open?

Round three will take the chosen answers into a fully responsive, motion-tested, copy-locked version of 03 and one alternate — probably a press-facing variant derived from 04's austerity. Reply with your three answers above and we'll land round three within the week.
