— what it is

A book club that doesn't die in a group chat.

doesn't die in a group chat
called out.
1984-2026.

Six to fifteen people who share taste. One shared spine for what you're all reading, watching, or listening to. Highlights sit next to the passage. Conversation attaches to the sentence it's about.

who share taste
so: M, J, T, S, and probably R if she's free.

You invite your people. Someone in the room shares the book, the essay, the podcast episode. As each member reads or listens, they mark what catches them — an excerpt, a paragraph, a timestamp in an audio file. Everyone sees everyone's marks.

a timestamp in an audio file

(keep reading keep reading)

No feed. No discovery tab. No strangers grading your takes. No algorithm picking what the room reads. The surface is whatever your six people are into this week, and nothing else.

— how it works · reading

The book stays pinned. The margins fill up.

Every room anchors to one artifact at a time — the piece of content you're all currently reading. Highlights from every member appear against the passages themselves, each in their own color. Comments attach to the excerpt or the sentence — not a floating thread that scrolls away from what it's about.

— the room

Small on purpose. Invitation by design.

Invitation by design
good. don't want randos.

Rooms are closed by default. Six to twenty members. Most rooms stay around a dozen people. Big enough that someone is always the interesting one. Small enough that you know every voice.

You can make a room public for read-only discovery (highlights and threads visible to the world, participation stays invitation-only), or keep it fully private for family, work, or the kind of conversation that should stay in the room.

— ownership

Your rooms aren't trapped here.

aren't trapped here
last reading app i used shut down in october.

Highlighter runs on Nostr — an open protocol where your identity is a key you own, your rooms live on a host you can change, and your highlights are portable events anyone can read. The technical details only matter if we go dark. If we ever do, your rooms move to another host and nobody loses a thing.

your rooms move to another host and nobody loses a thing
ok. that's unusually honest for a product page.
⌇
One key per reader. One protocol. No silo.

Your Nostr keypair is your identity. Your rooms are portable between hosts. Your highlights export to any compatible client. Built on NIP-29 groups and NIP-84 highlights. Interoperable by default.
