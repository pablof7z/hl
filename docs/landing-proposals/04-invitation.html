Act II · The Argument 02 / 04
— 01
What we made

The internet solved finding anyone to talk to. It never solved finding the particular few whose reading would make the conversation worth the time.

Highlighter is the smallest possible answer to that. An invite-only room, a specific book, a few people whose taste you trust, and a shared spine where highlights and arguments live next to the passage they are about.

No discovery surface. No algorithmic reading list. Nobody performing for strangers. The product does one thing slowly, and stops.

Act III · The Terms 03 / 04
— 02 · Terms of the room

Four things we will not do.

i
We will not build a feed.
The room's daily surface is whatever the room is reading this week. Not a ranked river of strangers. Never.
ii
We will not broadcast what is yours.
Private rooms stay private. Public ones stay read-only to non-members. Participation is by invitation, always.
iii
We will not hold your rooms hostage.
Rooms live on an open protocol. Keys and data are yours. If we ever go dark, the rooms can move without you losing a thing.
iv
We will not let the machines choose.
Your reading is not ranked, scored, or recommended by a model. The room decides what the room reads. The model's job is to extract, not to direct.
