Proposal C · Wireframe

The Track

A vertical navigable structure sorted by audio time. Always shows: chapters (when present), member clips, your own marks, and waveform-detected speech segments. When a transcript is available, paragraphs interleave inline. When not, the track stays dense with member clips and chapter markers — the experience never depends on transcript text. Clipping is purely time-based: tap "Mark In" to start a range, tap rows to extend it, tap "Save" to commit. Toggle the transcript on/off in the demo to see both modes.

Technical anchoring

  • !
    Transcripts are not always available. Many podcasts ship without machine-aligned transcripts. The Track works without one — chapters, clips, and waveform speech-detection still give the spine.
  • !
    Clipping is purely time-based. A clip is a (start_seconds, end_seconds) pair attached to the episode identifier (NIP-73 podcast:guid or :item:guid). Transcript text, if any, is decoration on top.
  • !
    Toggle "Transcript" off in the demo to see the no-transcript mode — clips and chapters become the dominant rows, with thin waveform ticks between.

UX notes

  • 1
    The track is always navigable. Every row maps to a precise timestamp. Tap any row to play from there. Member clips are always present; transcript is graceful enhancement.
  • 2
    Clip via Mark-In + Mark-Out. Tap the FAB to arm clip mode. The first row you tap becomes the start; the second tap (or the playhead position when you tap "Save") becomes the end.
  • 3
    Layer toggles up top. Show or hide transcript, chapters, clips. The track recomposes around what's available.
  • 4
    Member clips expand inline. Tap any clip card to see the comment thread + reply, without leaving the timeline.
  • 5
    The audio pill shows where you are. When transcript is present, the speaker name; when not, the timestamp. Either way, the playhead is the same — only the label adapts.
