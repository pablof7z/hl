The Clip Studio

A horizontal tape strip you scrub like film. Drop start/end markers on the fly with two taps. Active clip dominates the middle of the screen with adjustable handles + a note field. Drafts stack below. Other members' clips peek from a bottom drawer that you can pull up without losing your draft. Optimized for active producers.

Technical anchoring

  • !
    Mark In / Mark Out work on time alone. The clip is a (start_seconds, end_seconds) pair; transcript text is auto-extracted only if present. No transcript → the clip card shows "Time-only clip" and you write a note.
  • !
    Tape strip needs no transcript. Member-clip bands and avatars come from event tags (start/end times + author). Renders identically with or without transcript availability.

UX notes

  • 1
    The tape is your primary surface. Drag the playhead, see the whole episode at a glance. Member clips appear as soft gray bands so you can see where the conversation already is.
  • 2
    Two-tap clipping. "Mark In" at one moment, "Mark Out" at another. The active clip card slides up with the transcript text already filled. No mode switch.
  • 3
    Quick Clip = single tap. Drops a 30-second clip ending at the current playhead. Refine later if you want.
  • 4
    Drafts accumulate. You can keep clipping while listening; publish individually or in a batch. Published clips fade into the list with a "posted ↗" badge.
  • 5
    Room drawer is always one swipe away. See what others kept without losing your work. Tap any clip to jump audio there. Tap "+" to bookmark to your own vault.
  • 6
    Member avatars under the tape show which member clipped which moment — at a glance, you see whose attention is where.
