Proposal A · Wireframe

The Listening Room

The screen is anchored to audio time. Other members' clips appear as quote bubbles along a vertical river — past clips render fully, clips ahead of the playhead fade. Scrolling = scrubbing. Tapping a clip jumps audio there and expands the thread. Long-pressing the river drops a range selector and slides up the composer.

Technical anchoring

  • !
    Audio time is the only required substrate. The river is built from clip start_seconds, not transcript text. Episodes without transcripts render the same river — clip bubbles still anchor where they were created.
  • !
    The composer takes a time range first, transcript text second. If a transcript is present, the snippet is shown as a preview. If not, you write a note and save the clip with timestamps only.

UX notes

  • 1
    Audio time is the spine. Scrolling = scrubbing. The playhead is a thin horizontal line that follows your finger and locks to the moving audio when you let go.
  • 2
    Clips are voices in the margin. Each member's clip is anchored to the timestamp where it starts. As you listen, you literally see what's coming up.
  • 3
    Tap a clip = teleport. The audio jumps there, the bubble expands inline with the thread, and you can resume from that timestamp with one tap.
  • 4
    Clip = long press + drag. No "Mark In / Mark Out" button. Long-press anywhere on the river, drag two handles, release. The composer slides up with the transcript prefilled.
  • 5
    The dock is small and out of the way. Play/pause + a thin scrubber are the only persistent controls. The clip CTA is contextual — it appears under your finger.
  • 6
    Presence in the header. "3 listening now" creates the sense that you're in the room. Other members' clips landing in real-time would animate in.
