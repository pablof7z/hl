Wireframe proposals · iOS

A new podcast experience for Highlighter

Three first-principles redesigns of the iOS podcast surface — from the moment you tap an episode, through clipping a passage, to seeing what others in the room kept and joining the conversation. Wireframes only; UX over UI.

First principle 1
The artifact is the hero. Audio time is the spine of every interaction.
First principle 2
Clipping is a gesture, not a button. It happens where attention already is.
First principle 3
You are not listening alone. Other members' clips are part of the listening UI, not a separate tab.
First principle 4
Every clip is a seed. Conversation, bookmark, share — all reachable in one gesture.
Technical anchoring

Clips are (start_seconds, end_seconds) ranges attached to an episode identifier (NIP-73 podcast:item:guid:<guid> when available, falling back to podcast:guid:<guid>, then to the audio URL). They never require transcript text. Transcripts are optional decoration — most podcasts ship without machine-aligned transcripts, so the experience must be complete without one. Each proposal works with or without; toggle the demo to see both modes.

Proposal A

The Listening Room

"I am listening with friends."

A vertical river anchored to audio time. Other members' clips appear as voices in the margin while you listen — past clips above the playhead, future clips ahead. Scrolling navigates time itself. Most social.

Proposal B

The Clip Studio

"I am building my highlight reel."

A horizontal tape strip you scrub like film. Drop start/end markers on the fly. Drafts stack as cards you can adjust and publish in batches. Room clips peek from a bottom drawer. Most productive for active clippers.

Proposal C

The Track

"I am navigating structure."

A single vertical track of navigation markers, sorted by time: chapters, member clips, your bookmarks, and — when available — transcript paragraphs. Layer toggles let you show/hide each. Clipping is purely time-based: arm the clip FAB, tap two rows, save. Works identically with or without transcript.
