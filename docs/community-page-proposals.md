# Highlighter Community Front Page Proposals

**Content-Centric Model | Version 1.3 (2026)**
**Author: Grok (AI-generated draft)**
**Status: Under Review**

We've pivoted the entire community experience: the **main artifact** is now the full piece of content (book, article, podcast episode, YouTube video, research paper, etc.).

Highlights exist **only** as supporting teasers — short excerpts that pique interest and pull members into the deeper group conversation around the *entire* content. Standalone quote cards are gone. Every content item gets its own card/section with:

- Cover/image + title + source
- 1–3 highlight teasers (to spark curiosity)
- The primary unioned discussion thread around the whole piece

This maximizes growth/conversion: non-members see enough rich content + lively conversation to feel FOMO and request an invite; members get instant value and easy "invite friends to this exact discussion" loops.

## Proposal 1: "Content Library Feed" (Recommended – Most Practical)

**Vibe**: Clean, chronological library that feels like a smart, private book club shelf.

### Member View

- Top hero: Community name + member avatars + prominent **"Invite Friends to This Community"** button.
- Main feed: Vertical list of content cards (most recently shared at top).
  - Each card shows: Large cover image/title/source → "Shared by [Member]" → 2–3 highlight teasers (with "Read more in context" link) → Unioned discussion preview (2–3 latest comments) + "Join the full conversation" button.
- Floating action: "Share new content" (prompts URL/paste → AI suggests highlights).
- Sidebar/bottom nav: "Currently reading" carousel + AI weekly summary ("What the group got from this month's books").

### Non-Member View

- Same layout but blurred/teased. Hero says "Private community • 14 members discussing great books & ideas".
- Only the 4–5 most active content cards visible (highlights and comments truncated with "Members only" label).
- Strong top banner + floating CTA: **"Request Invite to Join the Conversation"** (pre-filled with "I'd love to discuss these pieces with your group").

**Growth power**: Every content card becomes a mini landing page that naturally invites sharing ("You'd love this discussion — want to join?").

## Proposal 2: "Discussion Rooms" (Conversation-Heavy)

**Vibe**: Feels like dedicated private salons, one per piece of content.

### Member View

- Hero bar: Community name + quick "Invite" + "Share New Content".
- Main area: Grid or list of "Active Rooms" — each room titled by the content piece (e.g., "Atomic Habits – Chapter 1–3" or "The latest Lex Fridman episode").
  - Inside each room card: Cover + title → 1–2 highlight teasers that act as "entry points" → Full unioned discussion thread (scrollable preview) + AI co-host summary ("Key takeaways so far…").
- Top filter: "All content" / "Unread by me" / "Most discussed".

### Non-Member View

- Hero identical but overlaid with "Preview only — join to participate".
- Rooms show cover + title + highlight teasers + 1–2 comment previews (truncated).
- Every room has a clear **"Ask to Join This Discussion"** button that sends a request directly to moderators/members.

**Growth power**: Non-members see real, ongoing conversations around compelling content and feel immediate pull to request access.

## Proposal 3: "Featured Content Hub" (Visual & Curated)

**Vibe**: Magazine-style hub. Emphasizes quality over quantity — only the best/shared-most content is highlighted.

### Member View

- Hero: Large rotating featured content piece (cover + title + "Currently buzzing in the group").
- Below: Masonry-style grid of other content cards.
  - Each card = big visual (book cover/podcast artwork) + title/source → Highlight teasers as subtle callouts → Unioned comment count + latest comment snippet.
- Quick actions on each card: "Add your thoughts" or "Cross-post this discussion to another of my communities".

### Non-Member View

- Hero shows one featured content piece with strong teaser highlights and comment previews.
- Grid limited to 6 cards with everything readable except full discussion.
- Prominent "This group is discussing [content] right now — request invite to join" banners throughout.

**Growth power**: The visual beauty + rich content previews make public links extremely shareable on social media, driving organic invite requests.

## Quick Comparison

| Proposal | Best for | Growth Strength | Conversion Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Content Library Feed | Everyday active groups | Fast scrolling + easy invite loops | Highest daily retention |
| 2. Discussion Rooms | Deep conversational groups | Strongest FOMO on live discussions | Best at showing conversation value |
| 3. Featured Content Hub | Smaller, curated circles | Most visually viral public previews | Strongest "wow" for non-members |
