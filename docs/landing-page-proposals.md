# Highlighter 2026 Landing Page Proposals

**Community-First Model | Version 1.2**

High-level concepts only (no pixel-perfect designs — just strategic direction, hero copy, structure, and vibe). All proposals are mobile-first, clean, and use the same brand palette (warm neutrals + soft accent color for highlights).

## Proposal 1: "Your Private Idea Circle" (Recommended – Most Aligned)

**Vibe**: Intimate, trusted, anti-social-media. Feels like a beautiful group chat that's actually smart.

### Hero (full-screen, minimal)

Background: Soft blurred image of 6–8 people's hands holding phones/books around a table, or abstract connected highlight cards floating gently.

**Headline:**
> Your friends. Your highlights. Your best ideas.

**Subheadline:**
> Private communities for the things you actually read, watch, and think about. Invite-only. No feed. No noise. Just signal.

**Primary CTA:** Start Your First Community → (pre-filled with "Book Club 2026" suggestion)
**Secondary:** Watch 48-second product video (one friend creating a community → sharing a highlight → magic union of comments).

### Below the fold (3 simple sections)

1. **"How it works in 30 seconds"** – 3-step horizontal scroll (Create → Invite friends → Share any highlight).
2. **"Real communities people love"** – 4 anonymized micro-examples ("Our 2025 Non-Fiction Club • 14 members", "Deep Tech Friends • 9 members") with fake highlight previews.
3. **Social proof strip**: "Built from the lessons of the original Highlighter (2018–2024)" + David King quote from shutdown note + early advisor logos.

### Footer CTA
"Ready for high-signal conversations again?" → big "Create Community (free)"

**Growth angle**: Immediately signals "this is for small trusted groups" → converts visitors who are tired of big public apps.

## Proposal 2: "Highlights That Travel With Your People"

**Vibe**: Dynamic and connected. Emphasizes the cross-community union mechanic as the magic.

### Hero

Background: Animated loop of the same highlight card "traveling" between three different community bubbles (color-coded).

**Headline:**
> One highlight. Multiple circles. Every conversation in one place.

**Subheadline:**
> Share the best parts of what you're consuming with any of your communities. All comments union automatically. Private by default. Public read-only for discovery.

**Primary CTA:** Try It With Friends → (opens a demo where you instantly "join" a fake 8-person community)

### Below the fold

1. Hero animation expanded into full demo flow (scroll-triggered).
2. **"Built for the way real friends actually talk"** – testimonials from imagined early users + one quote from LX Cast's LinkedIn comment about the original community magic.
3. **"From solo reading → shared understanding"** – before/after split (solo highlight vs. unioned discussion).

**Growth angle**: The hero animation itself sells the viral loop (share once → value multiplies across groups).

## Proposal 3: "The Anti-Feed Social Layer"

**Vibe**: Bold, slightly rebellious against modern social media. Clean and opinionated.

### Hero

Background: Dark mode by default with glowing highlight text.

**Headline:**
> Stop doomscrolling. Start highlight-scrolling with your people.

**Subheadline:**
> Small, invite-only communities where the only posts are the smartest excerpts from books, podcasts, videos, and the web. Discussions stay high-signal because the group is small.

**Primary CTA:** Create a Private Community (takes 12 seconds)

### Below the fold

1. **Trust bar**: "No algorithms deciding what you see. Just your friends and the best ideas."
2. **Three community archetypes** (with visuals): The 12-person philosophy circle • The 8-person founder book club • The family "what we're learning" group.
3. **"Public discovery, private depth"** – shows how a public read-only highlight can lead someone to request an invite.

**Growth angle**: Positions Highlighter as the thoughtful alternative to X/Instagram/LinkedIn feeds → attracts the exact user who wants smaller circles.
